    How Rational Works
    
          
        
    
        
    
          
    
            
    Expected Value (EV)
    
            
    Multiply each outcome's probability by its payoff, then sum. The option with the highest EV is mathematically the best choice on average.
    
            EV = Σ(probability × payoff)
          
    
          
    
            
    Base Rate Neglect
    
            
    Before trusting a signal, check how common the outcome is in the general population. A 99%-accurate test means nothing if the condition is 1-in-1000.
    
          
    
          
    
            
    Sunk Cost Fallacy
    
            
    Past investments are gone. Only future value matters. Would you make this same choice starting fresh today?
    
          
    
          
    
            
    Bayesian Thinking
    
            
    Update your beliefs proportionally to new evidence. Don't ignore it, don't overreact — nudge your estimate mathematically.
    
            P(A|B) = P(B|A) × P(A) / P(B)
          
    
          
    
            
    Survivorship Bias
    
            
    You only see the winners. Ask for the denominator — how many tried and failed? The graveyard is invisible but enormous.
    
          
    
          
    
            
    Kelly Criterion
    
            
    When you have an edge, Kelly tells you exactly how much to bet. Professionals use quarter-Kelly for safety.
    
            f* = (p × b - q) / b
          
    
          
    
            
    Sensitivity Analysis
    
            
    What if your probabilities are wrong? Sensitivity testing shows you how much they'd need to change before the best option flips.
    
          
    
          
    
            
    Calibration (Brier Score)
    
            
    Over time, we measure your predicted probabilities against actual outcomes. A Brier score of 0 is perfect; 0.25 is random guessing.
    
            Brier = (1/N) × Σ(forecast - outcome)²
